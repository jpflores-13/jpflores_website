  🌴 My name is JP Flores (he/him), and I recently completed my PhD in Bioinformatics & Computational Biology at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill with a Graduate Certificate in Innovation for the Public Good.

   🔬 During my PhD, I studied the role of 3D chromatin structure in response to environmental stress in the [Phanstiel Lab](http://phanstiel-lab.med.unc.edu/).

  🧠 I am a Fulbright Scholar in Milan, Italy 🇮🇹 working with [Dr. Michele Gabriele](https://www.gabrielelab.com/) at the San Raffaele Telethon Institute for Gene Therapy (SR-Tiget) and a HHMI Gilliam Postdoc in the lab of [Dr. Marissa Scavuzzo](https://scavuzzolab.org/) at Case Western Reserve University (Cleveland, OH, US).

  🗣️ I am a Co-Founder of the nonprofit organization, [Science For Good](https://sciforgood.org/), and I write about genomics and biology for [GeneBites](https://genebites.org/). I am also the host of [From Where Does It STEM?](https://fwdis.org/). 

  ✊🏽 Although science is what I want to do for the rest of my career, I am extremely passionate about empowering the next generation of scientists. I also co-organized the creation of the [Atlas of Inspiring Latinx Scientists](https://www.inspiringlatamscientists.org/), the [Atlas of Inspiring Hispanic/Latinx Scientists](https://www.fredhutch.org/en/about/about-the-hutch/faculty-development/atlas-of-inspiring-hispanic-latinx-scientists.html), the [Index of Inspiring Black Scientists](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/research/index-inspiring-black-scientists/), and an Atlas of Inspiring Indigenous Scholars (coming soon!).
  
  ⚖️ I love science policy and am a member of the [Scientist Network for Advancing Policy (SNAP)](https://snapcoalition.org/).

  🎓 I am a proud alum of Occidental College, a small liberal arts school in Los Angeles, CA.

  🐯 There, I played NCAA DIII baseball and majored in Cellular & Molecular Biology with a minor in Public Health. I also did research on the venom   of fish-hunting cone snails!
